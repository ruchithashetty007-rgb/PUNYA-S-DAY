# To My Dear Friend, Punyashree,

**Happy Birthday! ❤️**

🎉

I don't really know how to express everything that's in my heart.

If you know me well, you already know that I'm not someone who talks a lot. I'm usually the quiet person 🦉 in the room—the one who listens more than speaks, who overthinks before saying even a simple sentence. Sometimes I have so many things I want to say, but they never come out the way I imagine.

So today, instead of trying to say it all in person, I decided to write it.

Because sometimes, words written on paper are much braver than words spoken by an introvert.

It's hard to believe that it's already been **almost one year** since we first met in our **first year of Engineering, AI & Data Science**. We entered college as complete strangers, stepping into a new chapter of life filled with unfamiliar faces, classrooms, and experiences.

Back then, I never imagined that among so many people, I would find someone who would quietly become such an important part of my college journey.

Friendships don't always begin ✨ with something extraordinary.

Sometimes they begin with a simple "Hi."

Sometimes by sitting in the same classroom.

Sharing notes.

Walking after class.

Laughing over the smallest things.

Helping each other during assignments.

Those moments may have seemed ordinary at the time, but when I look back now, I realize those little moments became some of my favorite memories.

Thank you for being part of them.

💛

There's something I've never really told you.

You probably have many friends.

People who make you laugh, spend time with you, and create wonderful memories with you. And honestly, that makes me happy, because you're the kind of person who deserves to be surrounded by good people.

I don't know where I stand among all those friendships.

Maybe I'm just one small part of your engineering life.

Maybe just another classmate who became your friend.

And that's completely okay.

But I want you to know where you stand in my life.

Before college, I never really had many friends.

In fact, I never truly had even one close friend with whom I could share my thoughts freely. I was always the quiet one, standing in the background, watching everyone else make friends while I stayed inside my own little world.

Sometimes I wondered if I would ever find someone I could genuinely call a friend.

Then college happened 🪄.

We met.

Without either of us realizing it, simple conversations slowly turned into memories, and those memories slowly turned into a friendship that means more to me than I can explain.

You may never know how much those ordinary days meant to me.

A smile.

A conversation between classes.

Sharing notes before an exam.

Walking together.

Laughing over something silly.

Those moments might have been just another day for you...

But for me, they became some of the happiest memories of my college life.

As an introvert, I don't always know how to show appreciation.

I don't always message first.

I don't always know the right words.

Sometimes I care so much that I end up saying nothing at all.

Silence has always been easier for me than speaking.

But silence doesn't mean I don't care.

It simply means my heart speaks more quietly than my voice.

That's why I made this letter 🕯️.

Not because I wanted to give you something expensive.

Not because I wanted to impress you.

But because I wanted to give you something honest.

Something that came directly from my heart.

A small collection of thoughts that I may never have had the courage to say out loud.

Maybe one day we'll graduate.

Maybe we'll work in different cities.

Maybe life will become busy, and we'll meet less often than we do now.

But I truly hope that whenever you look back at your engineering days, somewhere among those memories you'll remember that there was a quiet girl named **Ruchitha** who was genuinely grateful to have met you.

This website is my little way of preserving one small piece of our friendship.

So whenever you open it again, I hope it reminds you that someone truly appreciated your kindness, your smile, and your friendship.

As you celebrate another birthday, I wish you nothing but happiness.

May your dreams come true.

May success always find its way to you.

May you stay healthy, confident, and surrounded by people who genuinely love and support you.

Never lose your beautiful smile.

Never stop being the kind-hearted person you are.

Thank you for every conversation, every laugh, every shared class, every little memory, and every ordinary day that became special simply because you were part of it.

A birthday cake becomes sweet for a day.

A gift may eventually wear out.

But I hope these words stay with you a little longer.

Because this letter isn't just a birthday wish.

It's a small piece of my heart.

Thank you for being one of the most meaningful friends I've ever had.

And thank you for making my first year of engineering so much more beautiful than I ever expected.

Once again...

**Happy Birthday, Punyashree.** 🎂❤️

🎊

May your life always be filled with happiness, peace, success, laughter, and countless beautiful memories.

Keep smiling.

✨

Keep shining.

And keep being exactly the wonderful person you are.

Because meeting you was one of the best things that happened during my engineering journey.

With lots of love, gratitude, and warm birthday wishes,

🧪

**Ruchitha ❤️**
